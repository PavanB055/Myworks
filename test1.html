The Town That Lives On

Pine Point was a small mining town in Canada's Northwest Territories—a place that once thrived but has since disappeared. Yet, its spirit lingers on in the hearts of its former residents and in the memories of those who once called it home. Welcome to Pine Point, an interactive documentary, resurrects this vanished community in a deeply personal and emotional way. By navigating a virtual map, visitors explore the lives and stories of those who lived there.

A Multimedia Scrapbook

The website combines photos, videos, and audio clips to create an intimate scrapbook of Pine Point. Clicking on a photo reveals a story—a fleeting memory, a heartfelt goodbye, or the joy of a small-town event. Each interaction immerses the user in the town's fabric, making them a part of its narrative.

The Creators Behind the Vision

The Goggles, a duo comprised of Michael Simons and Paul Shoebridge, brought Pine Point back to life. With backgrounds in design and storytelling, they were inspired to preserve a town that no longer exists, reflecting on what remains after physical places vanish.

The Power of Memory

Released in 2011, Welcome to Pine Point came at a time when digital storytelling was still finding its voice. The creators used the emerging tools of HTML5 and multimedia to weave together memories into an immersive experience. The cultural context of the period emphasized nostalgia and the preservation of community stories.

Technological Innovation

HTML5, CSS, and JavaScript were combined with multimedia tools to build the website. Archival materials, interviews, and photographs were digitized and stitched together, resulting in an interactive documentary that is as much about the future of storytelling as it is about the past.
